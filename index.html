The Incredible Mushrooms

Mushrooms are used extensively in cooking, in many cuisines (notably Chinese, Korean, European, and Japanese). Humans have valued them as food since antiquity.

Popular Items

Baby Bella mushrooms, also known as cremini mushrooms, are a more mature version of white button mushrooms, featuring a darker color and a richer flavor.

Oyster mushroom (also known as Pleurotus ostreatus, hiratake, or pearl oyster mushroom). Found in temperate and subtropical forests around the world, it is a popular edible mushroom.

Chanterelle is the common name of several species of fungi in the general Cantharellus, Craterellus, Gomphus, and Polyozellus. They are orange, yellow or white, meaty and funnel-shaped.

Shiitake, or black mushroom (also known as Lentinula edodes) is a macrofungus native to East Asia and mainland Southeast Asia, which is cultivated and consumed around the globe.

The largest and oldest living organism on the planet is a mushroom.
